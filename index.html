Health Crisis

KPH Surgeries Paralysed by Failing Air Conditioning — Opposition Calls It a "Patch and Pray" Catastrophe

Elective operations at Jamaica's busiest public hospital have been repeatedly cancelled due to a broken central A/C system — and the crisis is now stretching into a second week.

The central air-conditioning system serving two operating theatres at Kingston Public Hospital (KPH) has broken down, causing the cancellation and delay of elective surgeries across multiple specialties. The South East Regional Health Authority (SERHA) confirmed the disruption on Sunday, with KPH CEO Dwayne Francis issuing an apology to patients and their families.

Hospital management attributed the failure to the facility's ageing infrastructure — described as over 250 years old — and to the deterioration of critical components that are not readily available on the local market. Maintaining proper environmental conditions in operating theatres is essential to patient safety and infection control, KPH said, and without functioning A/C, surgeries cannot safely proceed.

"We are witnessing the same crisis, the same pattern and the same inadequate response. Surgeries are being cancelled. Lifesaving medical missions hang in the balance." — Opposition Health Spokesman Dr Morais Guy

Opposition Health Spokesman Dr Morais Guy — echoed by Dr Dayton Campbell — blasted the Government for what he called a systemic "patch and pray" approach to surgical infrastructure, noting that operating theatres at both KPH and the Bustamante Hospital for Children remain out of service due to recurring failures. The Government said plans to fix the units are "well advanced," with KPH assuring it will provide further updates.

State Minister: Jamaica's Rum Is an Economic Powerhouse — and Government Thinks "Much Bigger"

State Minister for Industry, Investment and Commerce Delano Seiveright called this week for far greater recognition of Jamaica's rum industry as a strategic pillar of national development. "When we look at rum we see agriculture, manufacturing, logistics, tourism, export earnings, intellectual property, rural livelihoods. Rum is one of the few products that tells Jamaica's story every time it is poured anywhere in the world." — State Minister Delano Seiveright

Renewable Energy

Energy Expert: Jamaica Can Achieve Over 90% Renewables — "Breaking the Grid"

A landmark energy lecture at the Jamaica Conference Centre has set out a bold blueprint for transforming the island's electricity system away from fossil fuels.

Dr Ramón Méndez Galain, speaking at the annual Maurice Facey Lecture Series at the Jamaica Conference Centre under the theme Breaking the Grid: Charting Jamaica's Path to Renewable Energy, told an audience this week that Jamaica can realistically achieve more than 90 percent renewable energy in its electricity mix.

The lecture comes directly in the wake of the June 5th island-wide blackout and mounting Opposition calls for the National Water Commission to shift critical infrastructure onto solar power. Dr Méndez Galain's central argument was that the economic and security case for Jamaica to transition is now overwhelming — and that the technology exists to do it.

Education

Portland's Cascade Primary Named Champion of Jamaica's School Nutrition Policy

A small school in western Portland has been singled out by the Health Minister as the best in the entire country for implementing the National School Nutrition Policy — out of more than 1,000 schools inspected.

Cascade Primary and Infant School in western Portland has been commended by Health and Wellness Minister Dr Christopher Tufton as Jamaica's outstanding champion of the National School Nutrition Policy. Tufton visited the school in Buff Bay Valley this week and praised it as the first school in Jamaica — and one of only five nationwide — to fully implement the policy.

"Cascade Primary and Infant School is an exceptional school. You are leading the country today in the school nutrition policy," Tufton told students and staff. "This little school on the hill in Portland is a champion out of 1,000 schools." The ministry inspected 202 schools over the past year, with Cascade Primary coming out clearly ahead of all others.

The recognition is a major source of pride for western Portland, a community that has historically received less attention than the parish capital Port Antonio. The achievement also underscores the potential of smaller rural schools when given focused support and policy direction.
