Health Crisis

KPH Surgeries Paralysed by Failing Air Conditioning — Opposition Calls It a "Patch and Pray" Catastrophe

Elective operations at Jamaica's busiest public hospital have been repeatedly cancelled due to a broken central A/C system, and the crisis is stretching into a second week.

The central air-conditioning system serving two operating theatres at Kingston Public Hospital (KPH) has broken down, causing the cancellation and delay of elective surgeries across multiple specialties. The South East Regional Health Authority (SERHA) confirmed the disruption on Sunday, with KPH CEO Dwayne Francis issuing a public apology to affected patients and their families.

Management attributed the failure to the facility's ageing infrastructure and to the deterioration of critical components not readily available on the local market. Maintaining proper environmental conditions in operating theatres is essential to patient safety and infection control — without functioning A/C, surgeries cannot safely proceed.

"We are witnessing the same crisis, the same pattern and the same inadequate response. Surgeries are being cancelled. Lifesaving medical missions hang in the balance." — Opposition Health Spokesman Dr Morais Guy

Opposition Health Spokesman Dr Morais Guy blasted what he called a systemic "patch and pray" approach to surgical infrastructure, noting operating theatres at both KPH and the Bustamante Hospital for Children remain out of service due to recurring failures. The Government said repair plans are "well advanced."

Education

Portland's Cascade Primary Named Jamaica's Champion School for Nutrition — Beating Over 1,000

A small school in western Portland has been singled out by the Health Minister as the best in the entire country for implementing the National School Nutrition Policy.

Cascade Primary and Infant School in Buff Bay Valley, western Portland, has been commended by Health and Wellness Minister Dr Christopher Tufton as Jamaica's outstanding champion of the National School Nutrition Policy. Visiting the school this week, Tufton praised it as the first in Jamaica — and one of only five nationwide — to fully implement the policy. Of 202 schools inspected over the past year, Cascade Primary came out clearly ahead.

"This little school on the hill in Portland is a champion out of 1,000 schools," Tufton told students and staff. The recognition is a significant source of pride for western Portland, a community that has historically received less public attention than the parish capital Port Antonio. The achievement also highlights the potential of rural schools when backed by focused policy support.
